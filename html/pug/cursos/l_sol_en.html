Solicitor

The diploma of higher professional technician in Solicitor is conferred to students who demonstrate, in this area:

a) develop and deepen the knowledge acquired;

b) apply the knowledge acquired, in order to show a professional approach to the work developed;

c) solve problems and construct and substantiate their own arguments;

d) collecting, selecting and interpreting the relevant information, which enables the support of the recommended solutions and the judgments issued, including in the analysis the relevant social, scientific and ethical aspects;

e) communicate information, ideas, problems and solutions to both specialist and non-specialist audiences;

f) develop skills that enable lifelong learning with a high degree of autonomy.

Profile of the study program

The profile of the study program comprises the development of technical-scientific knowledge and knowledge of a professional nature in the areas of Law studies

Access Requirements

Students who obtain a minimum grade of 95 points (on a scale of 0 to 200) in one of the following national entrance exams: Economics or History or Portuguese can apply.

Occupational profile of graduates

The degree in Solicitor is part of the training area of Business Sciences and Law, and its graduates are qualified to carry out a professional activity, within the scope of the profile of the study program.
