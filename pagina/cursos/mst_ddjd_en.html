Design and Development of Digital Games

The master's degree in Digital Game Design and Development is awarded to students who demonstrate the ability to:

a) Analyze and reflect on meanings, functions and impacts of digital game design and technologies in contemporary times, have an overview of the industry and demonstrate research capacity in the area.

b) Conceptualize, develop and validate pragmatic solutions by analysing the requirements and user experience.

c) Play a role as a designer, developer, producer, technical artist, critical journalist, teacher or researcher in a game design and development team and wider contexts (national/international; entertainment/serious games; research).

d) Use project management, version control, and design and development team management tools.

e) To underpin intrinsic motivation and the development of intellectual, practical, technical, organisational and communication skills throughout life, informing individual and collaborative praxis.

Profile of the study program

The DDJD Master's Degree aims to provide its students with a solid technical and scientific background, through interdisciplinary practice based on game design, articulating with scientific areas necessary for the development, evaluation and dissemination of digital games, responding to real needs of companies and institutions, reflecting the significant cultural and technological impact of digital games and thinking about the future of the area.

The general objectives are:

- Contribute with qualified senior staff (masters) in the field of design and development of digital games for the enrichment of the various areas of activity of the creative industries;

- Respond to social, economic, technological and cultural transformations by valuing specific resources and assets of the national territory;

- Promote research skills in the area of digital games and stimulate innovative projects;

- Promote qualified and creative entrepreneurship in the emerging segment of digital games.
